# [Get Started with CS] - Create and Publish Content

## Deploy Grouped Content
Large-scale, time-sensitive content management and publication is a challenging task. By pinning all the necessary entries and assets within a "Release," you can help streamline this task. All the pinned items are simultaneously published / unpublished to the environment of your choice when you deploy the Release.
- [Create a New Release](../content-managers/create-and-manage-releases/create-a-new-release.md)
- [Add Entry/Asset to a Release](../content-managers/create-and-manage-releases/add-entry-asset-to-a-release.md)
- [Deploy a Release](../content-managers/create-and-manage-releases/deploy-a-release.md)

Learn more about [Releases](/docs/content-managers/create-and-manage-releases/).

## Managing Trash
For up to 14 days following the date of deletion, trash keeps a backup of all objects that have been deleted. Before they are completely deleted, you can return removed objects to their original state.

Learn more about [Managing Trash](/docs/developers/manage-trash/).

## Common questions

### Who should use this page?
Content managers who need an overview of how to create, search, publish, and deploy content in Contentstack.

### Where do I go to learn how to create entries and manage assets?
Use the links under **Create Content**, and see **Learn more about Author Content**.

### How can I publish multiple items at once?
Use **Bulk Publish Entries** or **Bulk Publish Assets** under **Publish Content**.

### How long does Contentstack keep deleted items in Trash?
Trash keeps a backup of deleted objects for up to 14 days following the date of deletion.
