# [Second level navigation] - Teams

This page introduces the Teams feature in Contentstack and serves as a listing page for developers who need to understand, create, manage, and learn limitations and use cases for Teams when assigning roles and permissions.

## Teams

Contentstack introduces Teams, a feature created to simplify the assignment of roles and permissions. Teams are essentially a way to group users and spread roles across them. The actual permissions and roles work the same way as they do for individual users.

Teams makes it easier to manage roles and permissions by organizing users into groups. Instead of assigning roles individually or at the stack level, you can assign roles directly to a team. This ensures that all users within a team share the same set of role permissions

## Common questions

**How do Teams relate to roles and permissions?**  
Teams are essentially a way to group users and spread roles across them, and the actual permissions and roles work the same way as they do for individual users.

**Why use Teams instead of assigning roles individually?**  
Teams makes it easier to manage roles and permissions by organizing users into groups and allowing you to assign roles directly to a team.
